About Us

The Department of Zoology at Maa Manikeshwari University is dedicated to advancing knowledge and fostering excellence in the field of animal sciences. Established with a mission to provide high-quality education, cutting-edge research, and community engagement, the department has become a hub for budding zoologists, researchers, and conservationists.

Our Vision

To be a center of academic and research excellence in Zoology, promoting biodiversity conservation and sustainable environmental practices while nurturing the next generation of scientists.

Our Mission

  • Delivering comprehensive education in zoology, covering classical to modern aspects of animal biology.
  • Encouraging students and faculty to engage in meaningful research in areas such as wildlife conservation, taxonomy, ecology, physiology, and molecular biology.
  • Building awareness and responsibility towards environmental sustainability, biodiversity preservation, and animal welfare.
  • Collaborating with governmental bodies, NGOs, and research institutes to address pressing ecological and conservation challenges.

Key Focus Areas

  • Wildlife Conservation & Habitat Management
  • Taxonomy of Vertebrates & Invertebrates
  • Biodiversity Documentation & Conservation
  • Environmental Impact Assessment
  • Molecular Zoology & Genetic Studies

Academic Programs

The department offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programs designed to provide students with both theoretical knowledge and hands-on practical experience. Students are exposed to field trips, laboratory work, seminars, and workshops to enhance their learning.

Facilities

  • Well-equipped laboratories
  • Museum collections of various animal species
  • Research and field equipment
  • Access to journals, e-resources, and publications

Collaborations

The department actively collaborates with wildlife organizations, research bodies, and academic institutions to provide students with exposure to real-world conservation efforts and research projects.

    Understanding Different Mosquito Diversity to Assess the Burden of Mosquito-Borne Diseases in Kalahandi, Odisha

    This ongoing research project aims to document the diversity of mosquito species in Kalahandi, Odisha, and evaluate their role in transmitting diseases. The study will contribute to the development of targeted vector control strategies and public health interventions.
